The 12 Jyotirlingas

The twelve Jyotirlingas are the most sacred abodes of Lord Shiva. It is believed that Lord Shiva first manifested himself as a Jyotirlinga on earth on the night of the Aridra Nakshatra. These are the shrines where he appeared as a fiery column of light.

1. Somnath Temple

Prabhas Patan (Somnath), Gujarat

Considered the first of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the Somnath Temple is a magnificent and enduring symbol of faith and resilience, having been rebuilt multiple times after its destruction. It stands majestically on the western coast of India, its spire reaching towards the heavens, overlooking the Arabian Sea. The temple is a testament to the undying devotion to Lord Shiva.

2. Mallikarjuna Temple

Srisailam, Andhra Pradesh

Located atop the Nallamala Hills, the Mallikarjuna Temple is unique as it is one of the few shrines where both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha (for Goddess Bhramaramba) are present together. The temple complex is an architectural marvel, situated in a picturesque setting on the banks of the Krishna River, offering a deeply spiritual and serene atmosphere for pilgrims.

3. Mahakaleshwar Temple

Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh

This is the only Jyotirlinga that is 'Dakshinamurti', meaning it faces the south. Mahakaleshwar is a powerful and revered shrine located on the side of the Rudra Sagar lake in the ancient city of Ujjain. The 'Bhasma Aarti' (ash ritual) performed here before dawn is a famous and intense ritual that attracts devotees from all over the world, symbolizing the cycle of life and death.

4. Omkareshwar Temple

Khandwa (Omkareshwar), Madhya Pradesh

Situated on an island called Mandhata or Shivapuri in the Narmada River, the Omkareshwar Temple is a sight to behold. The island itself is shaped like the sacred 'Om' symbol (‡•ê), adding to its spiritual significance. The shrine houses the lingam, and the energy here is considered profoundly meditative. Pilgrims often also visit the nearby Mamleshwar temple, which is considered part of the same Jyotirlinga.

5. Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath, Uttarakhand

Set amidst the stunning, snow-capped peaks of the Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is one of the most revered and challenging-to-reach Jyotirlingas. The temple is accessible only for about six months a year due to extreme weather conditions. Part of the Chota Char Dhaam, this ancient stone temple evokes a powerful sense of devotion and awe, representing Lord Shiva's presence in the mighty mountains.

6. Bhimashankar Temple

Pune, Maharashtra

Located in the Sahyadri hills, the Bhimashankar Temple is surrounded by a dense wildlife sanctuary. The temple is built in the Nagara style of architecture and is the source of the river Bhima. The serene and natural environment, combined with the temple's ancient charm, makes it a perfect place for both spiritual solace and nature lovers.

7. Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Situated in the heart of Varanasi, the spiritual capital of India, this temple is perhaps the most famous of all Shiva temples. It stands on the western bank of the holy Ganges. It is believed that a single glimpse of the Jyotirlinga here is a soul-cleansing experience and that dying in Kashi grants 'moksha'. The temple, with its gold-plated domes, is the vibrant core of one of the world's oldest living cities.

8. Trimbakeshwar Temple

Nashik, Maharashtra

This temple is unique because its lingam has three faces, personifying Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu, and Lord Mahesh (Shiva). It is located at the source of the Godavari River, the 'Ganges of the South'. The temple is built of black stone and is an exquisite example of ancient Hindu architecture, set against the backdrop of the Brahmagiri hill.

9. Nageshwar Temple

Dwarka, Gujarat

The Nageshwar Jyotirlinga is believed to be the first Jyotirlinga on earth. The temple itself is a simple yet powerful structure, and it holds a special significance as a protector from all types of poison. The shrine is located on the coast of Saurashtra in Gujarat and features a large, impressive statue of a seated Lord Shiva outside the main temple complex.

10. Baidyanath Temple

Deoghar, Jharkhand

Also known as Vaidyanatha, this temple complex is revered as both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha. Lord Shiva is worshipped here as the 'divine physician' (Vaidya), and it is believed that offering prayers here can cure one of all ailments. The temple is a major pilgrimage site, especially during the Shravan Mela, when millions of devotees (Kanwariyas) carry holy water from the Ganges to offer to the lingam.

11. Ramanathaswamy Temple

Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

This southernmost Jyotirlinga is located on an island and is closely associated with the epic Ramayana. It is believed that the lingam here was established and worshipped by Lord Rama himself before he crossed the sea to Lanka. The temple is renowned for its magnificent architecture, especially its striking corridors, which are the longest among all Hindu temples in India.

12. Grishneshwar Temple

Aurangabad, Maharashtra

Referred to as the last or twelfth Jyotirlinga, the Grishneshwar temple is a fine example of 18th-century temple architecture. It is located near the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Ellora Caves. The temple, built of red rock, has a five-tier shikhara and intricate carvings. It is a powerful shrine that concludes the traditional Jyotirlinga pilgrimage circuit for many devotees.
