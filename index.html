FAQ

Everything You Need to Know

We've gathered the most common questions about India's most sacred temples to help you learn, from dress codes and timings to travel tips and guidelines.

What are the 12 Jyotirlingas?

The 12 Jyotirlingas are sacred shrines of Lord Shiva. They are: Somnath (Gujarat), Mallikarjuna (Andhra Pradesh), Mahakaleshwar (Madhya Pradesh), Omkareshwar (Madhya Pradesh), Kedarnath (Uttarakhand), Bhimashankar (Maharashtra), Kashi Vishwanath (Uttar Pradesh), Trimbakeshwar (Maharashtra), Nageshwar (Gujarat), Baidyanath (Jharkhand), Rameswaram (Tamil Nadu), and Grishneshwar (Maharashtra).

What are the 4 Char Dhaams?

The Char Dhaam ("four abodes") are four pilgrimage sites in India. They are Badrinath (Uttarakhand), Dwarka (Gujarat), Puri (Odisha), and Rameswaram (Tamil Nadu). It is believed that visiting these sites helps achieve 'moksha' (salvation).

What is the general dress code for temples in India?

Most temples require modest attire. Men are often advised to wear full-length trousers and shirts. Women are advised to wear sarees, salwar kameez, or long skirts/trousers that cover the shoulders, chest, and knees. Some temples in South India may require men to go shirtless and wear a 'mundu' (dhoti) and women to wear sarees.

Are non-Hindus allowed in all temples?

Entry rules vary. While most temples are open to everyone regardless of faith, some prominent temples, like the Jagannath Temple in Puri and the Kashi Vishwanath Temple (inner sanctum), have restrictions and may only permit entry to practicing Hindus.
